Delta

Eurocopter EC120 Colibri

Delta is a five-seat, single-engine, light helicopter. Jointly designed by Eurocopter (now Airbus Helicopters), China National Aero-Technology Import & Export
Corporation (CATIC), Harbin Aviation Industries Ltd (HAIG) and Singapore Technologies Aerospace Ltd (STAero) and developed at Eurocopter France's
Marignane Facility. Delta was assembled in Australia, while some other EC120s were assembled in France.

Epsilon

Kamov Ka-60

Epsilon is a 2-man crewed Russian helicopter first flown on December 24 1998.
She has a capacity of up to 15 passengers, 6 stretchers, 2,000 kilograms freight inside and 2,500 kilograms freight outside.
She has a length of approximately 15.50 meters, a rotor diameter of 13.50 meters, a height of almost 4.60 meters and a top speed of 310 kilometers per hour.

Nu

Boeing 747

Nu is an American wide-bodied commercial jet airliner and cargo aircraft, often referred to by her nickname of a Jumbo Jet.
Nu was one of the first wide-bodied aircraft produced.
Nu's model, the Boeing 747, is 150 percent larger than it's 'little sister' model, the Boeing 707.

Xi

Airbus A380

Xi is a double-decked, wide-bodied, four-engined jet airliner manufactured by European manufacturer Airbus.
She is the world's largest passenger airliner, and our airports have required facility upgrades to accommodate for her extreme size.
As of February 2017, Airbus had recieved 317 orders, and had produced and delivered 208 aircraft

Omicron

Douglas DC-3 Swissair

Omicron is a fixed-wing, propeller-driven airliner.
She has a cruise speed of 333 kilometers per hour and a range of 2,400 kilometers, which revolutionized air transport in the 1930's and 40's.
Civil production of Omicron's model, the Douglas DC-3, ended in 1942 after 607 aircrafts were made. Despite civil productions ending, when counting the C-47 (DC-3's military derivative), and Russian and Japanese-produced versions, upwards of 16,000 aircrafts were made.
After World War II, there were attempts to produce a 'super' DC-3, but these attempts were failed.

Pi

Boeing Dreamlifter

Pi is another wide-bodied cargo aircraft, and with a 1,840 cubic meter cargo hold, is the largest cargo plane in the world.
The Dreamlifter's exclusive use is transporting components of Boeing 787 Dreamliners to assembly plants from suppliers around the world.

Rho

Airbus A300-600ST (Beluga)

Rho is a version of the standard A300-600 wide-bodied airliner modified to carry aircraft parts and oversized cargo. She received the official name of the 'Super Transporter' early on, however, the name Beluga, after a whale, gained popularity and has since been officially adopted.
There are plans to produce an Airbus Beluga XL which will have 30% more capacity than the existing Airbus Belugas.

Sigma

Antonov An-225 Mriya

Sigma is the only of her model, which is a strategic airlift cargo aircraft designed and produced by the Antonov Design Bureau in the Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic within the Soviet Union during the 1980's.
She is powered by six turbofan engines and is the largest and heaviest airplane ever built, with a maximum take-off weight of 640,000 kilograms.
She was mothballed (retired in a storage area) for 8 years before being purchased by William's Aeronautics Services.
